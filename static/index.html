Wallet Classification Definitions

How SHI identifies behavioral patterns across wallet activity and funding relationships

🎯 Sniper

Wallets that enter positions very early (often within the first blocks of a token's launch) and typically exit quickly. Characterized by high turnover ratios and short holding periods.

Early entry timing Short hold duration High turnover
💎 Long-term Accumulator

Wallets that gradually build positions over time with minimal selling activity. These holders demonstrate conviction through consistent accumulation and low churn rates.

Gradual position growth Low sell activity Extended hold periods
🔗 Coordinated Cluster

Wallets that share common funding sources and exhibit synchronized trading behavior. Often indicates a single entity operating multiple wallets to obscure concentration.

Shared funders Temporal sync Similar patterns
💧 Liquidity Actor

Wallets with frequent interactions with liquidity pools (adding/removing liquidity). These actors provide market depth but may also extract value through LP strategies.

LP interactions Pool deposits Fee collection
🏦 Exchange-linked

Wallets with signatures matching known centralized exchange (CEX) patterns. High fan-out transactions and interaction patterns consistent with exchange hot/cold wallets.

High fan-out CEX signatures Batch transfers
🐋 Dormant Whale

Large holders with minimal recent transaction activity. These wallets hold significant supply but haven't moved tokens recently, representing latent sell pressure.

Large balance Low activity Long dormancy

Classifications are probabilistic and based on observable on-chain behavior. Confidence scores reflect the strength of detected signals.
